December 2022

Autonomous Reconnaissance System for Disaster Environments using Occupancy Grid Mapping and AprilTags Detection

I successfully contributed to the development of an autonomous search and rescue project utilizing Turtlebot3 Burger robots. The autonomous robot used for this project is Turtlebot3 burger and it is placed in an unknown area which is the simulated disaster environment populated with AprilTags that serve as the victim. The project involved designing and implementing an algorithm for real-time detection and localization of ArUco markers using a camera mounted on the robot. My responsibilities included algorithm development, testing, debugging, and data collection. I performed camera calibration using Zhang's method to estimate camera intrinsics and distortion coefficients and employed a non-linear optimization technique to minimize reprojection error. By integrating ROS packages like Cartographer, move_base, and explore_lite, the robot autonomously maneuvered and generated a comprehensive map of the environment, including the accurate identification and positioning of AprilTags. I utilized the GTSAM library to refine marker pose estimation, achieving an average reprojection error of less than 0.5 pixels. The project showcased a 90% detection accuracy in 30 test runs, accurately identifying 9 out of 10 ArUco markers.

April 2021

Autonomous Soccer Robots

During my engagement in the Robosoccer project, I played a significant role in advancing the field of autonomous soccer-playing robots. My contributions spanned across diverse domains, including pose estimation, path planning, firmware development, and circuit design enhancement.
For pose estimation, I successfully implemented the Extended Kalman Filter, enabling precise estimation of the robot's position on the soccer field. This played a crucial role in enhancing the robot's awareness and decision-making capabilities.
In the realm of path planning, I leveraged sophisticated algorithms such as A* and RRT* to design a comprehensive solution. By integrating the A* search algorithm as the global path planner and the RRT* algorithm as the local path planner, the robots were able to generate optimal high-level paths while navigating dynamically around obstacles in real-time.
To realize these functionalities, I delved into firmware development using embedded C/C++. This allowed me to develop robust firmware codes tailored specifically for the onboard microcontrollers (STM32F4xx) and the ESP8266 WiFi controller embedded within the robots. These codes facilitated seamless motion control, efficient data acquisition from rotary wheel encoders, and the implementation of intelligent control algorithms for various actuators.
Throughout my involvement in this project, I gained invaluable expertise in robotics, sensor fusion, path planning, firmware development, and electronic circuit design. This project served as an exceptional platform for me to showcase my skills and thrive in a highly competitive soccer-playing robotic environment.

November 2020

Integrated Circuit Design and Optimization for Soccer Robots: Power Board, Microcontroller Board, and Kicker Mechanism

I took the lead in designing the electronic circuitry for both the Power board and microcontroller board of the soccer robots, starting from the ground up. The entire process, including component assembly, was carried out in-house, ensuring meticulous attention to detail and quality control.
Furthermore, I tackled the challenge of optimizing the electronic circuit design for the robot's kicker mechanism. This involved the development of a specialized capacitor charge discharge circuit to efficiently power the solenoid kicker mechanism.
To achieve this, I introduced the LT3751 flyback controller chip, resulting in a remarkable 30% increase in the efficiency of the solenoid coil charge/discharge cycle. This significant enhancement played a vital role in elevating the overall performance and effectiveness of the robots during gameplay.

May 2019

Autonomous AirHockey Robots

I spearheaded the development of an engaging air hockey exhibit for Sporto-Mania gallery in the Robotics Gallery GCSC.
This interactive installation offers a captivating human vs machine experience, combining advanced technologies and strategic gameplay. Leveraging my expertise in computer vision and robotics, I successfully created a robust air hockey puck detection algorithm using HSV-based image segmentation and OTSU thresholding in OpenCV.
With an impressive frame rate of 120 frames per second, the system ensures highly accurate puck tracking using an overhead camera. Furthermore, I implemented a sophisticated finite state machine algorithm that dynamically adjusts the robot's play style between aggressive and defensive modes based on the puck's velocity and player behavior patterns.
This algorithm generates precise goal coordinates for a 4-Axis Epson Scara T6 series robot, which I programmed to execute accurate shots towards the opponent human player.
The exhibit's central computing unit developed in ROS efficiently handles image processing, trajectory generation, and robotic arm movement, providing seamless and immersive gameplay. Through meticulous design and execution, this air hockey project showcases my proficiency in computer vision, robotics, and interactive installations, making it a standout addition to my portfolio.

June 2022

Interactive Social Humanoid Robot - Pepper

In this project, I successfully developed and integrated advanced features into Pepper, the world's first social humanoid robot developed by SoftBank Robotics America. The primary focus was on enhancing Pepper's capabilities for human interaction, making it a fascinating exhibit.
Key Features:
Face Detection and Recognition: Implemented robust face detection and recognition algorithms, allowing Pepper to accurately identify faces of known people and even recognize basic human emotions. This feature greatly enhances Pepper's ability to interact and engage with individuals.
Multilingual Conversational Abilities: We developed conversational capabilities in two different languages - Hindi and English. Pepper can carry out meaningful conversations with users, catering to a wider audience and promoting inclusivity.
Touch Screen Interaction: Pepper is equipped with a touch screen interface, providing an additional means of interaction. Users can engage with Pepper through the touch screen, enhancing the overall user experience.
